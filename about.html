What are African Dwarf Frogs?

These are fully aquatic frogs that must live in an aquarium. Unlike other frogs, they cannot live on land as they will quickly dry out.

The term, African Dwarf Frog, actually encompasses four frog species:

  • Hymenochirus boettgeri (Tornier, 1896)
  • Hymenochirus boulengeri (De Witte, 1930)
  • Hymenochirus curtipes (Noble, 1924)
  • Hymenochirus feae (Boulenger, 1906)

Hymenochirus boettgeri is usually the most common species found in pet stores in America, but all four species are sold under African Dwarf Frog.

According to the information I have gathered, there is no difference in care between these species, but there has not been much research done on these frogs so who knows.

If you happen to own different species in the same tank, you may notice some frogs behaving differently or not interacting with other frogs.

These frogs are social creatures, although some may argue that their lack of intelligence hinders their ability to recognize other frogs. I do not have experience in this, but from reading other people's experiences, it seems it is highly recommended to have more than one frog, as they will feel more comfortable that way.
